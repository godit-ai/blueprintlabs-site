Key Research Insights

Preliminary findings from our ongoing research.

Insight #1: The "Wisdom of Crowds" Has Limits

Our analysis shows prediction markets exhibit systematic biases in specific contexts—particularly around partisan events, novel situations, and low-liquidity contracts. These biases create exploitable edges for quantitative traders.

Insight #2: Information Diffusion is Predictable

News travels through markets in predictable patterns. By monitoring information sources and measuring dissemination speed, we can front-run market movements by 15-45 minutes on average.

Insight #3: Market Structure Creates Edges

Fee structures, margin requirements, and liquidity constraints create systematic price distortions. Understanding these mechanisms reveals consistent arbitrage opportunities.
